About the lab: 

Our bioinformatics lab, established in July 2022 and affiliated with Guangzhou National Laboratory, focuses on advancing cancer research through large-scale bioinformatics and multi-omics data analysis. Over the past five years, we have aimed to build a comprehensive analytical framework rooted in tumor multi-omics data, providing molecular insights to accelerate precision oncology. Our key research areas include exploring genetic background-associated tumor heterogeneity, discovering new therapeutic strategies through integrative omics, and investigating tumor heterogeneity at the single-cell level. We also aim to identify biomarkers that predict drug responsiveness and uncover novel targets for immunotherapy and targeted cancer treatments. 

About the PI:
Dr. Jiao Yuan completed her undergraduate studies in Bioinformatics at Huazhong University of Science and Technology in 2010. She earned her Ph.D. from the Institute of Biophysics at the Chinese Academy of Sciences in 2015 under the supervision of Professor Runsheng Chen, focusing on the functional exploration of noncoding RNAs. Following this, she pursued postdoctoral research with Professor Lin Zhang at Penn Medicine, where her work centered on investigating the impact of genetic ancestry on tumor somatic alterations using computational biology and machine learning approaches. In 2022, Dr. Jiao Yuan was appointed as a principal investigator at the Guangzhou National Laboratory, and she also accepted a part-time professorship at Guangzhou Medical University. Aligned with the mission of the Guangzhou National Laboratory, her research now extends to investigating pathogen-host interactions, with the goal of addressing challenges posed by the pandemic.
